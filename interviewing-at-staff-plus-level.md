## Preparing the DS/A Interview
### Preparation
- Master your language's common utilities (e.g. Kotlin Collections, Random API).
- **Resource**: [Cracking the Coding Interview: 150 Programming Questions and Solutions](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/12544648-cracking-the-coding-interview) by Gayle L. McDowell
- **Practice Platforms**
  - [LeetCode](https://leetcode.com/)
  - [Grind 75 questions](https://www.techinterviewhandbook.org/grind75/)
- **Mock Interviews**
  - [Pramp](https://www.pramp.com/#/)

### Strategy
- Think out loud and discuss trade-offs (time/space complexity)
- Follow a structure: Listen > Edge cases > Brute force > Optimize > Walkthrough > Implement > Test
- **Key Resources**
  - [On Interviewing](https://adambennett.dev/2021/11/on-interviewing/) by Adam Bennett
  - [Common strategies for DS/A interview problems](https://github.com/ditn/interviewprep/blob/main/Common%20Strategies.md)

## Area of Expertise (Android)
Interviews may involve build an app from scratch, fixing bugs, or home assessments.

### Preparation
- Practice building a simple app with two screens (list & detail) using MVx architecture.
- Understand the company's tech stack (e.g. Jetpack Compose, DI, Threading)
- **Key Resource**: Paul Blundell's [Android Job Interview - Take-Home Test Cheat Sheet](https://blog.blundellapps.co.uk/android-job-interview-take-home-test-cheat-sheet/)

## System Design
### Strategy
- Ask questions first (functional/non-functional requirements like DAU, offline mode)
- Define network protocols (REST/GraphQL) and data models
- Discuss trade-offs openly

### Key Resources
- [Mobile System Design](https://github.com/weeeBox/mobile-system-design) GitHub project by Alex Lementuev (and his [YouTube mock interviews](https://www.google.com/search?q=https://www.youtube.com/c/AlexLementuev))
- [System Design Interview](https://bytebytego.com/) books by Alex Xu
- [Designing Data-Intensive Applications](https://dataintensive.net/) by Martin Kleppmann

## Behavioral Interview
### Strategy
- Prepare a table of your past projects reflecting on challenges, learnings, conflicts, and failures.
- Demonstrate how you acted as a multiplier for the team and impacted business outcomes.
- **Golden Rule**: Do not lie. Be honest but confident ("sell yourself").
